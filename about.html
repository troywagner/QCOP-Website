About

Welcome to QCOP
Philanthropy can be defined as "altruistic concern for human welfare and advancement", "charitable giving to human causes" or, put simply, "love of humanity". Queen's Conference on Philanthropy (QCOP) is one of Canada's only philanthropy–focused undergraduate conferences. Our mission is to inform, educate, and inspire delegates from leading universities across Canada. Through QCOP, we hope that students not only become more aware of the importance of philanthropy, but are inspired into philanthropic action of their own.

The theme for QCOP 2015 is CONNECT – an experience that challenges delegates to not only reflect upon important ideas surrounding philanthropy, but to "connect" to these principles in their daily lives. QCOP is a dynamic forum for the humanities, social activism and the understanding of both personal and corporate social responsibility.

Conference History
Since the first conference in 2009, QCOP has brought students together from across Canada to educate, inspire, and empower delegates to take action in philanthropy. Queen's Conference on Philanthropy, Canada's premier undergraduate philanthropic conference, was started by two Queen's undergrads who wanted to help other students realize that everyone is capable of making a difference. Now in its seventh year, QCOP continues to bring inspirational speakers and an impressive diversity of delegates.
